Safety Glasses for Bicycle Touring

Here in Australia, the sun is bright and the bugs are plentiful. Dirt roads abound where cars and trucks flick up tiny pieces of gravel. On a bicycle tour one of the most important things is to protect is your eyes. A pair of sunglasses is not a luxury, they are a necessity.

Branded bicycle sunglasses can put a significant dent in your budget, some pairs costing $200 or upwards. But there is a cheaper alternative.

This morning I bought myself a pair of tinted safety glasses for cycling touring. My local hardware store (Bunnings) had several to choose from, costing between $10 and $20. They are certified UV-resistant, lightweight and shatter proof. Safety glasses tick all the boxes, but cost ten times less.

The only downsides are that they are not fashionable, and in my experience they can fog up ever so slightly more when things get sweaty.

Sure, given a limitless budget - I’d prefer a branded pair. But if you are looking for a quality thrifty option for keeping the sun, flies and gravel out of your eyes as you ride: consider safety glasses.
